\noindent$O(f) = \Sigma_{i=0}^{n}$ $(i + log(i))$\\
\\
$= \Sigma_0^n (i) + \Sigma_0^n \left(log(i)\right)$\\
\\
$=\Sigma_0^n (i) + log(\Pi_0^n(i))$\\
\\
$\frac{n(n+1)}{2} + log(n!)$\\
\\
$= \frac{1}{2}\times n^2 + \frac{1}{2} \times n + log(n!)$\\
\\
\\
The dominant term in this formula is $n^2$, hence\\
\\
$f = O(n^2)$
